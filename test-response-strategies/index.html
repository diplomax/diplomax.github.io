Test Response Strategies

Dec 14th, 2014 1:29 pm

There are many strategies to help you maximize your performance on quizzes, unit tests, and final exams. The following provides information on three common strategies that you may find useful.

Five-Pass Strategy

This strategy has five steps or ‘passes’ for answering exam questions:

  1. Browsing Pass – scan the entire exam; note question formats, marks for each section, instructions
  2. First Answering Pass – answer questions you know the answer to and can complete quickly; maintain a brisk pace
  3. Second Answering Pass – answer questions left during the first pass that require more effort per mark; work the questions through until you experience an impasse or it is taking too long; maintain a steady pace
  4. Third Answering Pass – complete all partial answers; guess at any true/false and multiple choice questions you have left and attempt any questions still unanswered; all questions should now have full or partial answers
  5. Review Pass – check all answers and calculations as time permits

Three-Pass Strategy

This strategy has three steps or ‘passes’ for answering exam questions; is an abbreviated version of the five pass method:

  1. Overview – scan the entire exam; note question formats, marks for each section, instructions, and questions you can complete easily and quickly
  2. Second Pass – answer all questions you can complete without too much difficulty stopping when the question becomes too challenging or is taking too much time
  3. Last Pass – answer any questions that are left; guess and provide partial answers if necessary

Problem-Solving Strategy

This strategy has four steps to work through in answering a problem; the problem can be a word problem or a calculation problem:

  1. Understand the problem – What is the question? Do I have the information I need?
  2. Develop a Plan – Have I done something similar before? Can I break the question down into smaller parts?
  3. Carry out the Plan – Is my process logical? Have I shown all of the steps in the process? Have I included the correct units of measurement?
  4. Look Back – Have I answered the question? Does my answer make sense?

When writing an exam it is important that you use strategies that work for you – strategies that you understand and can use confidently. An exam is not the time to “try something new”.
